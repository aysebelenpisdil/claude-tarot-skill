# Spread Guide

This is a plain-language guide to choosing a spread. The authoritative
definition of every spread — card counts and position meanings — lives in
`references/spreads.json`; use `/tarot-info <spread>` to see the full
position map of any spread.

## Which spread, when

**Daily One Card** (`daily-one`, 1 card) — A single card as the macro energy
of your day, read as a psychological mirror rather than a prediction. Best
as a morning ritual or when you want one clear theme to sit with.

**Cause and Effect** (`cause-effect`, 3 cards) — For a concrete situation
you want to understand and act on. It builds a narrative from root cause to
present effect and closes with actionable advice. Reversed cards are never
skipped here; they are read as suppressed or blocked energy in the story.

**Reflection Cross** (`reflection`, 5 cards) — Inward-facing work: recurring
behavior patterns, shadow aspects, and the emotional triggers that feed
them. The final card names the lesson. Choose this when the question is
about yourself rather than a situation.

**Deep Intuitive** (`deep-intuitive`, 7 cards) — Expert mode. Seven densely
interwoven positions produce a layered reading that can be cognitively
overwhelming; the skill always asks for confirmation before starting and
will suggest the 3-card or 5-card spread if you are not ready. Pairs
especially well with the Thoth deck.

**Celtic Cross** (`celtic-cross`, 10 cards) — The classic full-picture
spread: atmosphere, opposing forces, past foundations, trajectory,
environment, hopes and fears, and a final synthesis. Choose it for a
significant question that deserves a complete examination.

**Horoscope** (`horoscope`, 12 cards) — One card per astrological house, a
panoramic scan of every life area. Available only if your books cover the
horoscope method; otherwise it appears disabled in the menu.

**Tree of Life** (`tree-of-life`, 10 cards) — The situation mapped onto the
ten Sephiroth of the Kabbalistic tree. Available only if your books cover
this method; otherwise it appears disabled in the menu.

## Decks

Every spread works with `--deck rws` (default) or `--deck thoth`. The Thoth
deck uses the same 78-card structure with its own card names (Strength
becomes Lust, the court runs Princess–Prince–Queen–Knight, Pentacles are
Disks). Card meanings always come from your own books in both cases.
